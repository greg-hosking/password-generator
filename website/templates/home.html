To prevent your passwords from being hacked by social engineering, brute force, or dictionary attack method, and to keep your online accounts safe, you should follow these guidelines:

• Avoid using the same password, security questions, and answers for multiple important accounts.

• Avoid using the names of your family members, friends, or pets, phone numbers, birthdates, social security numbers, etc. in your passwords.

• Use a password that has at least 16 characters, with at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special symbol.
